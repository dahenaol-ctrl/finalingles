Lesson 1 — First Conditional

Tourist Attractions in Beijing

Based on the brochure "Trip to China" — all descriptions use the First Conditional structure: If + present simple, will + verb.

🌊

Beihai Park & Imperial Gardens

"If you walk through Beihai Park, you will discover one of the finest examples of Chinese gardening art. If you take a boat ride on the lake, you will get a breathtaking view of the White Pagoda."

🏛️

Tiananmen Square

"If you stand in Tiananmen Square, you will feel the weight of Chinese history all around you. If you go early in the morning, you will witness the impressive flag-raising ceremony."

🏯

The Summer Palace

"If you spend a day at the Summer Palace, you will experience the beauty of a UNESCO World Heritage Site. If you climb Longevity Hill, you will see a panoramic view of Kunming Lake."

🔴

The Forbidden City

"If you visit the Forbidden City, you will be amazed by its 9,000 rooms and imperial history. If you arrive early, you will avoid the crowds and enjoy the architecture in peace."

🛕

Temple of Heaven (Tiantan)

"If you visit the Temple of Heaven, you will understand why emperors came here to pray for good harvests. If you explore the surrounding park, you will find locals doing tai chi."

⛰️

The Thirteen Ming Tombs

"If you travel to the outskirts of Beijing, you will encounter the magnificent burial sites of thirteen Ming emperors."

🐼

Beijing Zoo

"If you take your family to the Beijing Zoo, you will meet the famous Chinese giant panda in person."

🧱

The Great Wall of China

One of the New Seven Wonders of the World. Its winding path over rugged mountains totals around 5,000 km. In the eyes of most travelers — you haven't been to China if you haven't climbed the Great Wall.

⚔️

The Terracotta Army — Xi'an

Discovered in 1974, this UNESCO World Cultural Heritage Site features hundreds of life-size clay soldiers representing the army that unified China during the Warring States Period.

🌆

The Bund — Shanghai

One of Shanghai's most recognizable architectural symbols, showcasing colonial European buildings alongside modern skyscrapers along the Huangpu River.

🌃

Victoria Harbor — Hong Kong

Victoria Peak offers the iconic bird's-eye view of Hong Kong. At night, the Symphony of Lights from various buildings creates a spectacular show across the harbor.

Lesson 2 — Second Conditional

David's Travel Blog

Using the Second Conditional structure — If + past simple, would + verb — to imagine hypothetical travel scenarios across Chinese cities.

📍 Beijing

Dreaming of the Capital

Beijing holds centuries of imperial history within its walls. As I read about its majestic monuments, I can't help but imagine myself walking through them.

If I visited Beijing, I would spend three days exploring the Forbidden City and feeling the weight of 24 dynasties beneath my feet.
If I walked along the Great Wall at sunrise, I would feel like I was standing at the edge of history itself.
If I had a local guide, I would discover hidden hutongs and taste authentic Peking duck in a family restaurant.
📍 Xi'an

Meeting the Ancient Warriors

The Terracotta Army is one of the most extraordinary archaeological discoveries of the 20th century. Imagining a visit there fills me with wonder.

If I stood before the Terracotta Army in Xi'an, I would be speechless at the sight of thousands of unique soldier faces.
If I traveled to Xi'an, I would also explore the ancient city walls by bicycle at dusk.
If I had the chance to study ancient Chinese history on-site, I would understand the Qin Dynasty in a completely different way.
📍 Shanghai

The City That Never Sleeps

Shanghai is a city of contrasts — old and new, East and West. The Bund represents this duality perfectly, with colonial architecture facing ultramodern towers.

If I visited The Bund in Shanghai, I would photograph the skyline from every angle and watch the city transform from golden afternoon to electric night.
If I explored Shanghai's French Concession, I would find cozy cafés, tree-lined streets, and a fascinating blend of cultures.
📍 Hong Kong

The Pearl of the Orient

Hong Kong is a world of its own — a place where towering skyscrapers meet traditional fishing villages, and dim sum is served at midnight.

If I climbed Victoria Peak in Hong Kong, I would see one of the most spectacular harbor views in the entire world.
If I took the Star Ferry across Victoria Harbour at night, I would watch the Symphony of Lights dance across the skyscrapers and feel the magic of Hong Kong.

💬 Blog Comments — Answering the Unit 2 Questions


Q: From the 5 places mentioned, which ones would you like to visit the most? Why?

If I had the opportunity to visit any of the five places, I would choose the Great Wall of China without hesitation. If I walked along its ancient stones and looked out over the mountains, I would feel deeply connected to one of humanity's greatest architectural achievements. Additionally, if I visited the Terracotta Army in Xi'an, I would experience a once-in-a-lifetime encounter with thousands of warriors sculpted over 2,000 years ago.


Q: What would you like to do as a tourist if you had the possibility to visit China?

If I had the possibility to visit China, I would divide my time between history, food, and nature. I would visit the Forbidden City, climb the Great Wall, taste regional cuisines in Xi'an and Chengdu, and end my trip watching the lights of Hong Kong's harbor from Victoria Peak.

Travel Information

Essential Guide

✈️ Airlines

  • Air China (CA)
  • China Eastern (MU)
  • Avianca Colombia
  • Cathay Pacific
  • Korean Air (via Seoul)

📅 Best Dates to Visit

Spring: Mar–May (mild, cherry blossoms)

Autumn: Sep–Nov (clear skies, cooler)

Avoid: Golden Week (Oct 1–7) — very crowded

Festival: Chinese New Year (Jan/Feb)

🍜 Chinese Cuisine

  • Peking Duck (北京烤鸭)
  • Dim Sum (点心)
  • Hot Pot (火锅)
  • Dumplings (饺子)
  • Kung Pao Chicken
  • Mapo Tofu

🎎 Customs & Etiquette

  • Greet with a slight bow or handshake
  • Accept business cards with two hands
  • Remove shoes indoors (many homes)
  • Avoid tipping — it can be seen as rude
  • Red is a lucky color
  • 4 is considered unlucky (sounds like "death")

💱 Currency & Budget

Currency: Chinese Yuan (CNY / ¥)

Approx.: 1 USD ≈ 7.2 CNY

Budget stay: ~¥200–400/night

Mid-range: ¥500–1,200/night

🛡️ Visas & Entry

Colombian citizens require a tourist visa (L-Visa). Apply at the Chinese Embassy in Bogotá. Processing takes 4–7 business days. Validity: 30 or 90 days.

🌐 Connectivity

China's "Great Firewall" blocks Google, WhatsApp, Instagram, and YouTube. Download a VPN before entering. WeChat and Alipay are the main payment and communication apps.

🚇 Transportation

Beijing Metro: extensive and affordable (¥3–8/trip). High-speed trains connect cities (Shanghai in 5.5 hrs). Didi (Chinese Uber) is widely available.

✈️ Airlines

  • Japan Airlines (JL)
  • ANA (All Nippon Airways)
  • Avianca (via connection)
  • Korean Air (via Seoul)

📅 Best Dates

Spring: Late Mar–Apr (Cherry blossoms / Sakura)

Autumn: Oct–Nov (foliage season)

Winter: Dec–Feb for ski resorts and illuminations

🍱 Japanese Cuisine

  • Sushi & Sashimi
  • Ramen (various regional styles)
  • Tempura
  • Okonomiyaki
  • Wagyu Beef
  • Matcha desserts

🎎 Customs

  • Bow instead of handshake
  • No tipping — it is considered rude
  • Remove shoes before entering homes/temples
  • Silence on public transport
  • Carry cash — many places don't accept cards

✈️ Airlines

  • Thai Airways (TG)
  • Bangkok Airways
  • Qatar Airways (via Doha)
  • Emirates (via Dubai)

📅 Best Dates

Nov–Feb: Cool and dry season (best for tourism)

Apr: Songkran (Thai New Year water festival)

Avoid: Jun–Oct (monsoon/heavy rains)

🍛 Thai Cuisine

  • Pad Thai
  • Tom Yum Soup
  • Green/Red Curry
  • Mango Sticky Rice
  • Som Tum (papaya salad)

🙏 Customs

  • Wai (hands together bow) as greeting
  • Never touch someone's head (sacred)
  • Dress modestly at temples
  • The King is highly revered — be respectful
  • Bargaining is accepted in markets
Lesson 3 — Third Conditional

Looking Back: What If…?

The Third Conditional — If + had + past participle, would have + past participle — allows us to reflect on past hypothetical situations. Here are 10 reflections about a missed trip to China.

01If I had visited China last year, I would have climbed the Great Wall and fulfilled a lifelong dream.
02If I had studied Chinese before traveling, I would have communicated more effectively with local residents and understood the culture more deeply.
03If I had booked the 2-Day Beijing Highlights Tour, I would have seen the Forbidden City, the Temple of Heaven, and the Summer Palace in just 48 hours.
04If I had arrived in Beijing during spring, I would have experienced the city in full bloom and enjoyed mild, perfect weather for sightseeing.
05If I had attended the Kung Fu Show at Beijing Red Theatre, I would have been mesmerized by the incredible martial arts performance of "The Legend of Kung Fu."
06If I had visited the Terracotta Army in Xi'an, I would have stood face-to-face with warriors that had remained buried for over 2,000 years.
07If I had walked along The Bund in Shanghai at night, I would have photographed one of the most spectacular skylines in the world.
08If I had taken the high-speed train from Beijing to Shanghai, I would have saved hours of travel time and seen the Chinese countryside at 350 km/h.
09If I had downloaded WeChat and Alipay before entering China, I would have navigated cashless payments and digital communication without any problems.
10If I had climbed Victoria Peak in Hong Kong at night, I would have witnessed the Symphony of Lights — a breathtaking laser show across the entire harbor skyline.
